My Road to UX

  1. 01 Customer Service

    When people come to customer service, it is hardly ever with a smile on their faces. I have a special talent for making angry people smile.

  2. 02 Developmental Disorders

    Helping people with disabilities taught me to be patient and compassionate. This is the center of what UX represents.

  3. 03 Special/Reg Education

    Collaborating on multi-disiplinary teams to create and design individual curriculum for students with special needs taught me to be flexible and prepared me to positively impact a design team.

  4. 04 Art

    Sketching and painting is a natural step to branding and designing webpages. It is a gateway to more design based art and got me interested in vector images and creating them.

  5. 05 Web Design

    I studied the design process from understanding the problem, planning, research, testing usability, aesthetics, solution, iterations, and finally launch.

  6. 06 UX Design

    I transitioned into UX design because I have this desire to help people and wanted to do something just as meaningful that made me happy.

My Design Philosophy

My mother, who struggles with memory loss, will set out to complete a task, like paying a bill. She will get so confused and frustrated during the process that she can't continue and it ruins her day. This should not happen. I know that she is amongst thousands who go through this unfortunate scenario.

I believe technology is just a vehicle to get things accomplished. Tasks should be quick, easy, and convienent. People should be able to get on with their lives and not stuck on a website that is hard to navigate.

I want to help make things human centered so that this does not happen to anyone.
